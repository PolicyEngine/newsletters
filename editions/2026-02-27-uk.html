Reintroducing the two-child benefit limit

What would Reform UK's proposal mean for families, poverty, and the public finances?

Reform UK announced plans to reintroduce the two-child benefit limit, which Chancellor Reeves removed from April 2026 as part of the Autumn Budget 2025. We modelled the impact on government spending, poverty, and the income distribution.

Reintroducing the limit would save £3.6 billion in 2029-30, but increase absolute child poverty (BHC) by 1.9 percentage points—moving 300,000 additional children into poverty. The lowest-income households would lose the most, with the second decile losing an average of 1.3% of household income.

The distributional impact of student loan reform →

The debate between Martin Lewis and Kemi Badenoch raised a central question: who saves most under different student loan reforms? We built an interactive tool to find out.

Explore how cancelling the threshold freeze versus capping interest at RPI affects graduate repayments across the income distribution. Graduates starting on £30k save up to £7,609 under the freeze cancellation, while higher earners save up to £40,952 under the interest cap.
