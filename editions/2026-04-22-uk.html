New Interactive Tool

Energy price shock: distributional impact and policy options

Estimate the distributional impact of energy price shock scenarios driven by the Middle East war on UK households, with electricity and gas breakdowns, and model five policy responses.

Key findings

• Under no policy intervention, a price cap rise adds between £174/year (+10% scenario) and £2,800/year (Q1 2023 peak scenario) to the average household energy bill
• A flat £400 transfer costs £12.8bn but covers less than half the £1,045/year average hit under a +60% scenario
• A shock-matching flat transfer fully offsets the average household hit but costs the exchequer £5.6bn for a +10% scenario
