New Release

policyengine.py v4: one package, household to population

We’ve launched policyengine.py—more integrated, more reproducible, and AI-native. One package covers household calculations and population-level microsimulations, with pinned data artifacts from HuggingFace for full reproducibility. Plain dictionaries in, results out—designed for both human analysts and AI agents.

import policyengine as pe

result = pe.us.calculate_household(
  household={...},
  output=["employment_income",
    "eitc", "household_net_income"]
)

New Dashboard

Utah 2026 tax changes: income tax cut and expanded CTC

Two Utah bills take effect in 2026: SB60 cuts the flat income tax rate from 4.5% to 4.45%, and HB290 raises Child Tax Credit phase-out thresholds—up $6,000 for single filers and $7,000 for joint filers. Explore household-level impacts, statewide effects, and congressional-district breakdowns.

Under the hood

Recent releases in our open-source US model and microdata pipeline:

Rules →
  • CHIP premiums modeled for 17 states with enrollment take-up and federal/state cost attribution.
  • Child care assistance programs added for CT, NJ, SC, and VA.
  • SSI state supplements added for CT, DC, FL, HI, IN, KS, NM, SC, and TX.
  • 529 plan contribution tax benefits across 24 states; Social Security retirement benefit calculation chain.
Data →
  • EITC calibration rebuilt on coherent IRS SOI TY2022 targets with ~326 new per-state and per-child targets.
  • ACA Marketplace ETL with state-level bronze-plan selection targets for APTC recipients.
  • SIPP-imputed vehicle count and value inputs added for asset-tested programs like TANF.
  • SPM thresholds corrected to use the Betson three-parameter equivalence scale.
