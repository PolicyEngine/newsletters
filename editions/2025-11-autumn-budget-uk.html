Autumn Budget 2025: Full analysis now available

Since Chancellor Rachel Reeves delivered the Autumn Budget, we've published detailed analysis of each provision. Today we're launching two new tools that bring it all together.

New interactive tools

Explore how all budget provisions interact. Compare impacts by income decile, constituency, and year through 2029-30.

See how the budget affects you from graduation to retirement. Track cumulative effects of threshold freezes over 45 years.

Two-child limit removal →

The government will remove the two-child benefit cap from April 2026. Our analysis finds:

  • Cost: £2.9bn in 2026-27, rising to £3.6bn by 2029-30
  • Child poverty (BHC) falls by 13-18%
  • Income inequality (Gini) falls by 0.6%
  • 560,000 families gain, 4.4% of population

Fuel duty freeze →

Five-month freeze with staggered reversal of the 5p cut through March 2027. Our analysis finds:

  • Cost: £3.5bn in 2026-27
  • Average household gains £109
  • Income inequality falls by 0.21%

April 2025 tax changes →

Combined impact of employer NI, CGT, SDLT, and Council Tax reforms now in effect:

  • Total revenue: £31.8bn
  • Average household impact: £1,133
  • Top decile pays £2,754; bottom decile £808

Income tax threshold freeze extension →

Personal Allowance (£12,570) and Basic Rate threshold (£37,700) frozen through April 2031—the longest freeze in decades.
