4 policies to watch in today's Autumn Budget

Chancellor Rachel Reeves delivers the Autumn Budget today at 12:30 PM. Here's our analysis of four policies the government may announce:

Two-child benefit limit →

The two-child limit restricts Child Tax Credit and Universal Credit child elements to the first two children. Our analysis finds:

  • Repeal would cost £2.5 billion per year
  • 300,000 children lifted out of poverty
  • Child poverty rate falls by 1.2 percentage points
Income tax & NI reforms →

The Times reported the Chancellor considered raising income tax by 2pp while cutting NI by 2pp and extending threshold freezes. Our analysis finds:

  • Combined package would raise £6.9 billion in 2026-27
  • Workers face offsetting changes; pensioners pay only the IT increase
  • Households earning under ~£58k gain; higher earners lose up to £1,748
VAT thresholds →

The Telegraph reported the Chancellor is considering raising the VAT registration threshold. Using synthetic firm microdata, we find:

  • Raising threshold to £100k would cost £371 million in 2026-27
  • Would exempt ~48,000 firms from VAT registration
  • Lowering to £70k would raise £642m but add 109,000 firms
Mansion tax →

The Times reports a levy on homes worth over £2 million. We mapped 2024 property sales as a proxy for where high-value homes are concentrated:

  • Three central London constituencies account for 21% of high-value sales
  • London overall accounts for 45-46% of affected properties
  • Top 10 constituencies (all London/commuter belt) hold 36%
