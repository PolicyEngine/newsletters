Happy Tax Day

Two new tools for a day about what taxes—and benefits—really do

While filers hit the April 15 deadline, we're releasing a dashboard for the Working Parents Tax Relief Act and Cliff Watch, a new calculator that shows where the safety net penalizes earning more.

New Dashboard

Working Parents Tax Relief Act

Rep. Kristen McDonald Rivet's Working Parents Tax Relief Act boosts the EITC for families with children under 4. Our calculator shows the maximum EITC rises by $5,500 for a household with one young child, $11,000 for two, and $16,500 for three—and lets you score the reform for your own household.

Explore the dashboard →

New Interactive Tool

Cliff Watch: benefit cliffs, state by state

Cliff Watch traces a household's net resources—market income plus SNAP, TANF, WIC, free school meals, refundable credits, and healthcare value, minus taxes—across an earnings curve and across all 50 states and DC. Build a household, watch the cliffs appear, and see where earning a dollar more leaves a family worse off.
